About me

My name is Martín de la Cruz and I am a software developer student at Universidad ORT - Uruguay. At the moment, I am working for a big IT Company altough I am not coding or involved in the developement of an application. That is why I am looking for my first job experience developing software.

I am currently a Global Operations Manager, leading a team of eleven people. I closely monitor different "Key Performance Indicators" in regards of our global service, coordinate the operations directly with the client, create knowledge articles and support my team in any kind of aspect. Another important point to mention regarding the application I support, is the exposure to many different user interfaces I have. Part of my job is to ensure they are friendly to end-user for a correct navigation.

According to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR), my English level is "C1 Advanced". I have studied English for six years and other two of real daily practice on a work environment. Apart from expressing my ideas with a high level of fluency and accuracy, I am able to communicate effectively in a wide range of topics. On the other hand, my mother tongue is Spanish.

In 2018, I decided to start learning how to code using the language 'C'. Of course, it was not the best idea as it is a very complex language for a beginner. However, my first variable declaration, my first loop and my first "real program" was made in 'C'. I really enjoyed video games so my first motivation was to build some of them. After learning the basics in 'C', I quickly changed to 'C++' in which I could finish my first little game. Making use of pseudo-random numbers, the computer set 5 different random numbers from 1 to 6 in a specific order. The goal was to guess these numbers in their correct position. The player only had a limited amount of attempts and the system would show you how many numbers were correct and how many of them were on the correct position. I also programmed a hang man game in 'C++'. Finally I moved to the game engine 'Unity' and built two extremely buggy Android games. I could perfectly install them in my mobile phone though.

Finally, in 2022 I decided to start the university and officially learn software developement. I am very grateful not only for the opportunity but also for how motivated I feel about this.
